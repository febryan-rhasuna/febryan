Centrifugal Pump Performance Analysis

A CFD-based study to optimize centrifugal pump efficiency for mining dewatering operations through impeller diameter reduction and semi-circular trailing edge trimming.

Using ANSYS Fluent simulations, the project evaluated how different impeller diameters and trailing edge trimming sizes affect head, output power, and efficiency. The goal was to meet the >90% efficiency requirement for mining dewatering pumps.

Mining dewatering pumps require extremely high efficiency (>90%) to minimize operational costs. Conventional impellers often underperform without precise optimization of their geometry.

Tested four impeller diameters (204, 199, 194, 189 mm) and four trailing edge trimming sizes (2, 4, 6, 8 mm) under seven mass flow rates. Identified configurations delivering the highest efficiency and head using CFD simulation at 2900 RPM.

Key Features

  • CFD analysis using ANSYS Fluent
  • Tested impeller diameters from 189 mm to 204 mm
  • Semi-circular trailing edge trimming from 2 mm to 8 mm
  • Efficiency consistently above 90% across configurations
  • Best result: 8 mm trimming, 91.24% efficiency at 60 m³/h flow rate
  • Clear Best Efficiency Point trends for flow vs. head performance
